The Long body and the Haudenosanee

These are native tribes that plan ahead for seven generations. Haudenosanee means literally 'those who build' but also refers to the concept of the Long Body: an extension of the individual to include larger family, partners, allies and belongings, all that shapes and nourishes their community. These alliances were sometimes forged in violent battles against common enemies, like in the Iroquois Wars. But in times of truce, I like to think that the extended community builds for the common good and shares know-how, commodities and affection. I like to cook and feed people and share a few recipes with the remote parts my own long body who cannot taste them day to day.

My apologies to the native Indians if my ramblings are not a true reflection of their nature.
