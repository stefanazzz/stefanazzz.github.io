Earth Structure and Dynamics, part II

Notes on Earthquake Seismology


This second part of the module will focus on the aspects of earthquake seismology in the dynamic Earth. We will start by the mechanical aspects which allow to describe the state of stress, and how it relates to deformation of elastic solids. Then we’ll look at how stress induces failure, and how tectonic slip on faults occurs during earthquakes. We will define how earthquakes can be measured through the use of magnitude scales, seismic moment, and how these relate to the energy released by seismic sources. Finally, earthquakes radiate elastic waves which propagate at either short or long distances in the Earth. We will learn the mathematical representation of waves in solids, and how seismic waves can be used to infer the structure of the global Earth.


S. Nielsen, Durham, January 2021.
