Bokeh Plot

The stress state remains fixed in this example. However the stress tensor expresses the state of stress in a particular referential; therefore will vary upon rotation of the referential. Likewise, traction will vary depending on the surface direction.

The blue line represents a surface, and the dotted line represents the normal vector to such surface. The red arrow shows the traction applied to the blue surface. You can manipulate the graphic with the slider. This allows to rotate the surface, and to show the corresponding change in traction and in the stress tensor components.

Note how the diagonal elements of the stress tensor go to zero whenever the traction vector becomes parallel to the normal vector. This happens twice: when the normal vector is parallel to the major principal stress direction (110o) and when the normal vector is parallel to the minor principal stress direction (90o).
