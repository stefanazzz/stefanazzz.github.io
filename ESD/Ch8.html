8.3 Earth structure


Earth properties variation with depth are due to three main contributions: pressure, temperature and composition. First, the density of the minerals will increase as the pressure (compressive stress) increases with depth and lithostatic load. On the other hand, higher temperature at depth may contribute to increased buoyancy (relative density loss). Contrary to water and ice, most minerals and rocks have a lower density once melted than in their solid state. Indeed in solids, with few exceptions like ice, the atoms are arranged in a very compact lattice which fits more atoms per unit volume than in a random liquid state. In addition, mineral phases undergo transitions, as their stable lattice structure depends on the pressure and temperature conditions. For example, Olivine ((Mg, Fe)2â¢SiO4), which is one of the major constituents of the Earth mantle, is believed to be in a relatively loosely-packed lattice known as Î±-phase in the upper mantle. However it transforms into the Î²-phase or wadsleyite at pressures roughly corresponding to 410 km. A further transformation into spinel or ringwoodite occurs at about 520 km. Finally, at 660 km the Î³-phase should transform into two different high pressure phases perovskite and magnesiowustite. However phase transformations are not sufficient alone to explain all of the observed changes, for example, observations of seismic waves, whose trajectories are shown in a very simplified version shown in figure 8.9, and whose arrival times are depicted in figure 8.10). Some features of indicate, indeed, that a compositional layering is also necessary (change in chemical components), in addition to the phase transitions (crystal structure re-arrangement of the same components). Certainly the composition of the Earth core is quite distinct, and, though such composition remains unsure, the inferred wave speed and density are compatible with a high iron content, plus other element(s) with lower density (possibly sulphur, oxygen, silicon, potassium or hydrogen) in a proportion of 10 to 15%.
